COVID-19 in the U.S

Coronavirus (COVID-19) is an illness caused by a virus that can spread from person to person. As of July 30, cases had occurred in all 50 U.S. states, the District of Columbia, and all inhabited U.S. territories except American Samoa.

This site provides some visualizations of the COVID-19 data in the U.S. The COVID-19 data used are from Johns Hopkins University. The auxiliary data are obtained from United States Census Bureau (Link 1 and Link 2).

Cases vs. Deaths by State (as of July 30)

This chart shows the total cases and deaths by state as of July 30. Each state is represented by a circle. States are grouped into geographical regions and marked in different colors and can be used as a filter. The size the circle indicates the population of the state. The dashed lines mark different fatality rate. Mouseover a circle shows the actual number of cases and deaths of the state.
